Selected work

01

Weak Signal Extraction in Noisy Data

Identified meaningful signals in datasets dominated by background noise, even without per-event ground truth.

Methods: mixture models, likelihood fitting, feature selection

  • Built probabilistic mixture models with signal and background components.
  • Used maximum likelihood estimation and feature-based selection strategies.
  • Validated with proxy metrics such as signal yield, width, and dataset stability.

Impact: improved reliability of weak-signal detection under low signal-to-noise conditions.

Industry relevance: fraud detection, anomaly detection, weak supervision.

Selected evidence from project work

  • A 2022 mass fit produced a narrow peak near 1019 MeV with about 1.9k signal candidates over a smooth background component.
  • Signal yield, fitted width, and stability across selections were used as proxy quality metrics when event-level truth was unavailable.

02

Time-Series Modelling with Bias Correction

Inferred latent dynamics from noisy temporal data affected by measurement resolution and selection bias.

Methods: time-dependent modelling, calibration, joint fitting

  • Built time-dependent probabilistic models across multiple datasets.
  • Corrected for resolution effects, acceptance bias, and noise dilution.
  • Used calibration samples and control datasets to reduce systematic error.

Impact: increased parameter stability and reduced systematic uncertainty across datasets.

Industry relevance: retention modelling, churn analysis, causal inference.

Selected evidence from project work

  • Run 1 and Run 2 proper-time acceptance curves were modelled separately rather than assuming a single shared temporal response.
  • Control-channel data, MC acceptance ratios, and GB-reweighting were combined to reduce bias in time-dependent inference.

03

Machine Learning under Distribution Shift

Improved model robustness when training and real-world data did not follow the same distribution.

Methods: GBDT, reweighting, feature-distribution validation

  • Trained Gradient Boosted Decision Trees and compared feature distributions.
  • Applied gradient-boosting-based reweighting before retraining.
  • Validated alignment at the feature level to improve downstream generalisation.

Impact: reduced dataset mismatch and improved downstream model generalisation.

Industry relevance: domain adaptation, production ML, dataset shift handling.

Selected evidence from project work

  • Train/test BDT response comparisons were used to verify score separation before downstream threshold tuning.
  • Reweighting was treated as part of the validation loop whenever calibration and signal-like samples differed in kinematics.

04

Sensor Signal Modelling and Reconstruction

Converted raw sensor signals into more accurate measurements while separating different signal types.

Methods: waveform analysis, pulse-shape discrimination, statistical reconstruction

  • Modelled the signal chain from energy deposition to electronic readout.
  • Analysed waveform structure and extracted discriminative pulse-shape features.
  • Improved resolution and classification through statistical signal modelling.

Impact: improved measurement resolution and signal-type discrimination for complex sensor data.

Industry relevance: sensor analytics, signal processing, IoT, embedded systems.

Selected evidence from project work

  • Beam-test efficiency maps and Geant4 shower simulations were combined to study how non-uniform light collection affects reconstructed energy.
  • Energy resolution was fitted from 1 to 34 GeV, with a baseline constant term reported at about 0.63% for the beam-test map.

05

Model Calibration and System Optimisation

Reduced bias and non-linearity in measurement systems through iterative calibration and parameter tuning.

Methods: correlation analysis, calibration loops, simulation comparison

  • Analysed variable correlations and system response behaviour.
  • Applied transformations, calibration strategies, and simulation comparisons.
  • Improved measurement accuracy through repeated refinement loops.

Impact: reduced system bias and improved accuracy in non-linear measurement settings.

Industry relevance: system calibration, digital twins, engineering optimisation.

Selected evidence from project work

  • A clear correlation between the response parameter a and energy was tracked across operating points instead of being treated as noise.
  • That trend was then used to define a rotation-based correction for a more stable normalized response.
